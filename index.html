Tribute to Sushant Singh Rajput
(21 January 1986 – 14 June 2020)

"A talented actor who left an indelible mark on the Indian film industry."

Biography

Early Life and Education

Sushant Singh Rajput was born to Krishna Kumar Singh and Usha Singh in Patna, Bihar. His father retired as a technical official from the Bihar State Handloom Corporation in Patna. Gulshan, the youngest of five siblings, was his nickname. Mitu Singh, one of his four sisters, was a state-level cricketer.In Patna, he attended St. Karen's High School. Following his mother's death in 2002, Rajput's family relocated to Delhi, where he completed his intermediate studies at Kulachi Hansraj Model School.

Rajput was a voracious reader who was fascinated by astrophysics and won the National Olympiad in Physics. He was admitted to the Delhi College of Engineering (later called Delhi Technological University) to study mechanical engineering for a Bachelor of Engineering degree. According to Rajput, he had no interest in engineering but his family forced him to, leaving him disappointed. Instead, he aspired to be an astronaut and, later, an air force pilot, but he was also interested in Bollywood, having a crush on Shah Rukh Khan.

Career

Sushant Singh Rajput has an illustrious career in the Indian cinema business, displaying his talent, versatility, and love of acting. He began his career in the entertainment industry as a television actor, becoming well known for his role in the TV series "Pavitra Rishta." His portrayal of the main character, Manav, struck a chord with the public, establishing him as a promising talent.

In 2013, Sushant made his Bollywood debut with the film "Kai Po Che!" directed by Abhishek Kapoor. The movie was well-received, and his performance earned him critical acclaim and a Filmfare Award nomination for Best Male Debut. This marked the beginning of his successful transition from television to the big screen.

Sushant Singh Rajput has starred in a variety of films of various genres over the years, demonstrating his flexibility as an actor. He played a variety of characters and took on difficult roles with conviction. "Shuddh Desi Romance" (2013), "PK" (2014), "M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story" (2016), "Kedarnath" (2018), and "Chhichhore" (2019) are some of his significant films.

One of his most acclaimed performances came in "M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story," where he essayed the role of the legendary Indian cricketer Mahendra Singh Dhoni. Sushant's portrayal was widely praised for capturing the essence of Dhoni's persona and garnered him several awards and accolades.

Apart from acting, Sushant was an avid learner and had a keen interest in various subjects, including astrophysics and technology. He often expressed his curiosity and love for learning. He actively pursued his interests, even enrolling in a course at the prestigious St. Xavier's College in Mumbai.

It's important to remember and celebrate his contributions to cinema, acknowledging the talent and passion he brought to each role. Sushant's career serves as an inspiration for aspiring actors and reminds us of the transformative power of storytelling through the medium of film.

Personal Life

Sushant's personal life, particularly his relationships, has always been a hot topic among his fans and the media. He openly proposed to Ankita, his Pavitra Rishta co-star, and they began dating on the set of Pavitra Rishta. They were in the news again when they began living together in 2012, when they secretly married, but both parties denied it. Sushant announced in 2016 that they will marry in December, however they both ended their romance before that. Ankita revealed to the media in 2021 that Sushant wanted to pursue his career and that their relationship was over.

Sushant was still in the news after his relationship ended due to his relationships with many actresses. When he first started working on Raabta in 2017, he was rumoured to be dating Kriti Sanon, however this was later proven to be a rumour and disputed by both actors. The next year, he was back in the news for his support of Padmavat producer Rajput Karni Saini.

Later in 2018, Sushant was rumoured to be dating Saif Ali Khan's daughter and actress Sara Ali Khan; they both neither accepted nor denied this and remained apart, however in 2019, Sushant was said to be seen with Rhea Chakraborty (famous Bollywood actress). Rhea agreed that they were dating after his death, and Sushant was unwilling to take anti-depression medicines since he believed Yoga. Sushant was planned to marry in 2021, according to his father, but he didn't notify him about the bride.

Aside from his love life, while filming "Chanda Mama Door Ke," Shushant, who already had an interest in Astronomy and Astrophysics, began reading literature on the subject and accumulated a collection of 125 volumes on Physics. He purchased numerous additional books because he was an avid reader who was also interested in Cognitive Science and Behavioural Economics.

Social Impact and initiatives

The ‘Kai Po Che’ actor believes in ‘Right to Education’ and thus, plans to provide free education for the underprivileged and doing his bit for education and student welfare. Sushant told IANS about the initiative: “My team is working on the idea where we are selecting a group of schools, and we will take a merit test of the kids. If the kids pass the exam, we will take care of their education cost for a year. Next year, they again have to sit for the test to get the free education. This is the first step and will work on other models. But I think this process will give kids a new excitement and encouragement to study and make them more competitive.”
The actor also spoke about the importance of education: “My mother always taught me that we educate our children not to become a doctor or engineer, but the impact of basic education reflects on their thought process and decision making. So even if you are getting into a creative profession like acting, your basic education will open your mind enough to think analytically.Therefore, I have taken this small step towards providing basic education to deserving children.”
